# Financial services platform manages 5,000+ hosts with continuous compliance visibility

A financial services platform operates in a complex, regulated environment with thousands of endpoints across macOS, Windows, and Linux. The team needed an open, flexible approach to endpoint visibility — one that wasn't constrained by proprietary limitations and could feed directly into the security tooling they already operate.

## At a glance

- **Endpoints with Fleet visibility:** 5,000+ hosts across macOS, Windows, and Linux
- **Primary requirements:** Cross-platform observability, deep osquery integration, SIEM/SOAR integration
- **Key integrations:** Jira, Zendesk, and SIEM/SOAR systems
- **Previous solution:** Workspace ONE

## The challenge

The team struggled with the complexity of Workspace ONE and limited visibility across non-Mac environments. Linux servers, in particular, were a notable blind spot, and the proprietary nature of the previous tool made it difficult to integrate telemetry into the security workflows the team relied on.

The team needed a platform with deep, open, queryable endpoint data — and one that could plug into the systems they already used to investigate and respond to issues.

## The solution

Fleet gave the team a foundation built on open, transparent endpoint visibility. The ability to review source code and use open practices was described as the "cornerstone" of the team's confidence in their stack.

Full osquery integration provides granular, SQL-based access to endpoint state across operating systems. Fleet's API and webhooks let the security team feed real-time device telemetry directly into the SIEM/SOAR systems where they already operate, turning endpoint data into a first-class signal in their detection and response workflows.

## The results

- **Continuous compliance visibility:** Real-time osquery telemetry gives the team a constant view of endpoint state across operating systems, replacing point-in-time audit cycles.
- **Seamless migration:** By running Fleet in parallel with legacy systems, the team ensured a "behind-the-scenes" transition with minimal disruption to employees.
- **Custom SIEM dashboards:** The security team builds custom dashboards by integrating device telemetry directly with their SIEM/SOAR systems for rapid incident response.

## Why Fleet

For this team, the key advantage is open, queryable endpoint data that fits naturally into their security stack. Fleet provides cross-platform telemetry, deep osquery integration, and flexible API access — without the constraints of proprietary tooling.
