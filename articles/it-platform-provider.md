# IT platform provider automates patching across thousands of Mac, Windows, and Linux devices

An all-in-one platform for identity, MDM, and procurement needed a programmable layer to serve as the "secret weapon" for their own automated services. With thousands of cross-platform devices to keep patched and compliant — and a growing set of customer-facing automations to power — the team needed an API-first system to build on.

Fleet helped the company replace ManageEngine with a unified, GitOps-driven approach to patching and device telemetry across Mac, Windows, and Linux.

## At a glance

- **Endpoints:** thousands across managed accounts
- **Primary requirement:** robust API and patch management automation
- **Key integrations:** Brew and Winget
- **Previous solution:** ManageEngine

## The challenge

Before Fleet, the company relied on ManageEngine to handle cross-platform patches and verify device state. As the platform's automation ambitions grew, the team needed a more programmable foundation—one they could plug directly into the customer-facing services they were building, rather than a separate system to operate alongside them.

Managing cross-platform patches and verifying device state in real time across thousands of endpoints was a significant hurdle, especially when reliable, programmatic device data needed to feed automation downstream.

## The evaluation criteria

The team focused on three priorities:

1. **Robust, GitOps-friendly API** — Drive patching and configuration through version-controlled, programmatic changes.
2. **Cross-platform package management** — Native integration with Brew and Winget to manage software consistently across Mac, Windows, and Linux.
3. **Real-time device telemetry** — Verify device state instantly to power downstream automation and remediation.

## The solution

Fleet gave the team a programmable layer they could build on. Its deep GitOps integration allowed the company to develop its own automation and patching logic on a secure, auditable foundation, while a unified system manages Mac, Windows, and Linux through tools like Brew and Winget.

By using Fleet's API, the team drives patching and device verification programmatically, making real-time device state a first-class input to their internal automation pipelines rather than something that has to be polled or manually chased down.

## The results

Fleet improved automation, consolidation, and operational efficiency.

- **Automated app patching:** Using the API, the team programmatically updates Fleet-maintained apps via GitOps.
- **Consolidated stack:** Replaced 4+ legacy tools with one system, reducing vendor sprawl.
- **Real-time device telemetry:** Live device data is available to the team's internal systems for proactive monitoring and remediation across the fleet.

## Why Fleet

For this company, the key advantage is having a programmable, API-first platform they can wire directly into their own services. Fleet provides the GitOps foundation and cross-platform telemetry needed to deliver automated patching and device verification at the scale their customers expect.
